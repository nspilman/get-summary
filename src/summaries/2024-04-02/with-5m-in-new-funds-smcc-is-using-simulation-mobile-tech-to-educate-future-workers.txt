The article discusses how Southern Maine Community College (SMCC) has received $5 million in funding to enhance its educational programs. Specifically, $1 million has been allocated for a new mobile learning lab van for STEM training, while $4.1 million will go towards establishing a state-of-the-art health care simulation lab. The focus is on providing students with realistic, hands-on experiences to simulate various health care environments and meet the growing demand for skilled health care professionals, especially in light of the shortage exacerbated by the COVID-19 pandemic. Additionally, the Mobile Learning Lab vans will offer training programs in STEM, advanced manufacturing, and welding across the state. The article highlights SMCC's dedication to meeting the evolving needs of Maine's workforce through these initiatives.
