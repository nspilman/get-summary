The article discusses how banks in Maine are adapting to changing customer needs and market dynamics. Androscoggin Bank has opened new offices in different locations, including one in Portsmouth, N.H., and another in Guilford, to better serve clients and grow its customer base. Additionally, the bank is planning to close a retail branch in Auburn to optimize resources. Other banks like Bangor Savings and Kennebec Savings are also expanding their footprint by opening new branches. The focus is on finding the right locations for branches that offer convenience, ease of access, and a good fit for the community. As the banking landscape evolves with technological advancements and changing customer preferences, branches are being redesigned to offer more private services and employ fewer staff who are cross-trained. The article highlights the competitive nature of the industry, with banks aiming for efficiency and strategic growth through branch expansions and consolidations.
