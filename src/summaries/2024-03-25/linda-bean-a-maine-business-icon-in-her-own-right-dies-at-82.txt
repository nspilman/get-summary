Linda L. Bean, a prominent business figure in Maine and granddaughter of L.L.Bean Inc. founder Leon Leonwood Bean, passed away at the age of 82. Veronika Carlson, president of Linda Bean's Perfect Maine Hospitality, confirmed her passing. Linda Bean owned a variety of successful businesses including a company that sold and shipped lobster nationwide, several restaurants and lobster stands, vacation home rentals, an event venue, and two general stores. In addition to her business ventures, she was a philanthropist and political candidate, having run for the U.S. House of Representatives from Maine's 1st Congressional District. She also served on the boards of various nonprofit organizations. U.S. Senator Susan Collins, among others, mourned her death, highlighting Bean's love for Maine and her efforts to promote Maine lobster.
