The article discusses musician David Byrne's involvement in a discussion about the economic impact of dam removals along the Penobscot River in Maine. Byrne, known for his work with Talking Heads and as a writer, highlighted the positive effects of undamming the Penobscot River and how it has led to increased environmental health and economic benefits in the region. The restoration project involved the cooperation of different groups and the removal and restoration of dams, leading to improvements in fisheries and tourism. Byrne emphasized the importance of balancing the benefits and disadvantages of dam removal in different contexts, showcasing success stories where dam removal has led to job creation and improved property values near rivers.
