Merton Weed Jr., a man from Norway, has been sentenced to two years in prison and three years of supervised release for PPP loan fraud. He was also ordered to pay $253,646 in restitution for submitting fraudulent applications to the Paycheck Protection Program, obtaining $243,745 through falsified information. Weed pleaded guilty in October and was sentenced on May 31 by U.S. District Judge Nancy Torresen in Portland. His fraudulent claims included false monthly payrolls and employee numbers for non-existent businesses, supported by falsified bank records. The FBI investigated and arrested Weed in February 2023.
