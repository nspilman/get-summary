The article discusses the ongoing debate about the regulation of short-term rentals in various cities and towns in Maine. Key points include:

- Portland has required registration of short-term rental units since 2017, with annual renewals.
- Bar Harbor implemented regulations in 2021, setting a cap on the number of rentals allowed by non-resident landlords due to a shortage of workforce housing.
- Other municipalities such as South Portland, Freeport, Kennebunkport, Falmouth, and Cape Elizabeth have also enacted various regulations including mandatory registration.
- Smaller towns are considering how to regulate short-term rentals to address local needs and concerns.

The public is asked whether they support regulation of short-term rentals and given multiple options to express their opinion on this issue.
